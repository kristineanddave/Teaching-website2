About Me...

I earned my Bachelor of Arts degree with an Honors Specialization in Visual Arts from the University of Western Ontario in London, Ontario, Canada, which is also where I was born and raised. In my undergrad, I focused on printmaking and analog photography although my current practice has moved more towards painting and ceramics. From there, I moved to Kingston, Ontario where I earned my Bachelor of Education degree from Queens University. In 2015, I completed my Masters of Arts in Education- Visual Arts Education, from the University of Nebraska. From training and practice in the feild, I have realized that there can and will never be one perfect art curriculum because art educators need to alter the materials and methods taught dependent upon the specific group of students; who they are, where they are in the world, what their interests are etc. Objectives within an art program will never be static and therefore strong art teachers must be dynamic.
Art has always come naturally to me and is a subject I genuinely enjoy to both teach and practice. After a practicum experience in Buenos Aires at an International School, I knew that teaching internationally was the perfect career for me, and, fit with my motto - life is not meant to be lived in one place. I have now travelled to around 60 countries and with every country I visit, I want to see more. I believe it is our responsibility to see and experience how other people, fortunate and not so fortunate, live. My multicultural Canadian upbringing and experiences around the world have made me extremely tolerant, open-minded, inspired by others, curious, and eager to discover more.
